My Projects

Bankers Dashboard - Fifth Third Bank

Overview

As the lead UX/UI designer embedded within Fifth Third’s enterprise applications team, I drove the end-to-end user experience for multiple initiatives over the course of a year. One of the flagship projects focused on streamlining the workflow for internal banking employees, specifically those responsible for managing outbound customer calls and maintaining client relationships. The goal was to reduce friction in their day-to-day operations while providing more contextually relevant tools to support customer interactions. The resulting dashboard experience included personalized scripting based on customer profiles, real-time issue tracking, a dedicated notes section, and an adaptive layout tailored to each call’s context and needs.

Please note: Mockups shown are not representative of the final product due to intellectual property and patent considerations.

Target Users

  • Internal banking employees, specifically those making regular calls and maintaining customer accounts

Key Contributions

  • Led the design and iteration of low-fidelity wireframes using Sketch, adapting designs to meet evolving technical and business constraints
  • Developed user research plans, synthesized findings, and delivered actionable recommendations to inform both UX strategy and feature prioritization
  • Created user personas representing core banking employee workflows to align design decisions with real-world usage patterns
  • Delivered style guide components and early design system assets to support consistency across internal tools
  • Provided accessibility considerations to ensure internal platforms were inclusive and compliant with WCAG standards

Main Challenges

Lack of Documentation

When starting on the dashboard, there was no documentation on the designs done by the previous designer so most of the dashboard had to be rebuilt from scratch

Restrictive Tools

The tools and frameworks used to develop the dashboard were very restrictive in terms of functionality that would to the best UX and UI

Limited Usability Testing

Because of the tight timeline, it was difficult to get time to do usability testing on the mockups during the design process

Full Process

Requirements Gathering

My first project as a senior product designer at Fifth Third was Bankers Dashboard. This was a pre-existing project in the middle of a complete redesign under the enterprise applications pillar of design. I was introduced to the project via the lead product manager on the project and got an overview from her on what the project was and some time to gain my bearings and get comfortable with the design.

Resource Gathering

The product had no previous artifacts and a fairly tight timeline for launch of the application. The designer who had previously been working on the product did not leave any files associated with the product as they worked on everything in their own local drive and we could not gain access to any of those files so I did the following.

  • Worked with my product and engineering stakeholders to gain access to the current application and walked through it myself, conducting a heuristic analysis of the current state to understand where it currently was.
  • I combined this analysis with the PNGs of mockups the previous designer had sent over to my product stakeholders to identify the areas the designer was working on improving as well as trying to match up visual styles as the product stakeholders were very attached to the visual treatment the previous designer had created.
  • With what I was able to collect from stakeholders, I recreated the design as closely as I could in Sketch, checking in with Product to identify any features that I was missing in my recreation of the design.

High Fidelity Flows Part 1 and Part 2 + MVP Definition

Once I was as close as possible to what had previously been created, I worked with Product to understand what was remaining for their original MVP that they had decided on for launch before I started and worked on those components.

I was able to complete the original designs needed for MVP launch. While I wasn’t able to conduct any kind of iterative usability research, I was able to establish two sessions with users, one to confirm the MVP design in its most final state and another to reconfirm the design as the first round of research showed us a major usability issue in the original flow that needed to be addressed before launch.

From the small amount of usability testing I was able to conduct, we introduced a new post-launch MVP to address some of the concerns our users had brought up as well as functionalities and features I was able to point out weren't optimal for the user experience, but didn't have time to fix before launch.

Documentation Hand-off

After about a year and a half as the dedicated design resource for Bankers Dashboard, my priorities shifted as the role of Senior UX Producer was created for me and I stepped into design operations/design strategy work full time. With this change, I made sure to provide as much documentation as possible to make sure designs and development were consistent across all bank applications and engineering/product had as many resources as possible to continue with future iterations of the product.

  • I established a shared folder system in the cloud that documented the work that I was doing for the product that was easily accessible by anyone who would need to work on that product so that all research and designs were together in one place and not hidden away in any local drives where only one person could access them.
  • I established point of contact on the design system team as well as provided development with style guide and branding resources for implementation of the dashboard
