My Projects

CDPH Work - Deloitte

Overview

Over a two-year period, I led all UX and UI efforts across ten major digital health initiatives for the state of California, one of the largest and most complex engagements in the state’s portfolio. During this time, I helped launch two brand-new applications that served over 20,000 users, while also maintaining and enhancing the usability of several high-impact legacy systems affecting more than 500,000 users. These applications supported a wide range of public health workflows and user groups across the state.

**Due to the sensitive nature of the projects, full visuals cannot be shown.**

Target Users

  • General users across all offices for client included nurse practitioners, clinicians, doctors, genetic counselors, expecting moms, new moms, general public looking for public health guidance (i.e. COVID guidelines), etc

Key Contributions

  • Designed low- to high-fidelity wireframes in Sketch for both net-new applications and iterative improvements to legacy systems
  • Developed detailed user research plans, synthesized insights, and delivered actionable recommendations across all ten projects
  • Created user personas representing key roles including clinicians, nurse practitioners, genetic counselors, and members of the general public
  • Built and delivered front-end templates using HTML/CSS/SASS to accelerate development and maintain consistency across platforms
  • Provided accessibility audits and WCAG-compliant design recommendations to ensure inclusive access across all applications
  • Partnered closely with multi-disciplinary teams across health departments, engineering, and state leadership to ensure each product met both technical and regulatory requirements

Main Challenges

Restricted Access to Users

Because of the nature of most applications, access to users was difficult to gain for in-person usability testing

Client Bias

Many clients were both clients and users of the systems which led to bias

Restrictive Tools

The tools and frameworks used to develop applications were very restrictive in terms of functionality that would to the best UX and UI

Full Process

Requirements Gathering

For each project per office for the client, I established initial meetings conducted with the key stakeholders to understand the current application (or lack thereof) and learn the stakeholders' needs. Due to the high level of variability between clients, I tried to establish a baseline of the following:

  • What, if any, previous work had been performed for the project and where any existing resources were
  • Where the application lived (i.e. web, mobile app, public facing, private facing, etc)
  • Who were the clients/target users for the project
  • What was the timeline
  • What are products' goals and what needed to be done

High Fidelity Mockups

For most projects, the main need was for design for a product on a very strict timeline. Most frequently, this meant gathering as much information up front to be confident enough to jump into creating high fidelity mockups for an application. In the case of four major projects under GDSP (Genetic Disease Screening Program), the ask was to create a digital application based off a fully physical, pre-existing paper system. One such example of this was for Genetic Counseling Licensure (GCL). With this project, we were given a more open-ended timeline that allowed for low fidelity wireframes as well as usability testing with the final system in development.

User Research (When Applicable)

For most projects, it wasn't possible to meet with users in order to conduct any kind of user research. The exception to this was with GCL where we were able to fit in usability testing with the final system in a development environment.

  • For this study, we had originally planned to conduct in-person, on-site moderated usability sessions with 7 participants. The sessions would be 60 minutes each, with the user walking through the system in the way they expected it to function, giving their open and honest feedback about how the process was in general as well as how it compared to the existing paper system. The users were clinicians who had their genetic counseling licenses and had completed the paper process to receive them in the state of California.
  • Because of the timing of the sessions, the original plan was disrupted because of the initial outbreak of COVID in San Francisco (where the client site was). With a set release date that could not be adjusted, I ended up training a few of my product managers on how to conduct the usability sessions, providing them with the script that was written and general advice on what to say/not say, etc. We ended up conducting the 7 original sessions in about 3 days that way, identifying a huge usability issue with one of the screens that was able to be fixed before launch.

Interactive Prototypes (When Applicable)

With many of the small projects that had design needs, there were limited front end development resources available to help implement the designs that were created. In order to further help out on the client, I helped to provide HTML and CSS/SASS prototypes of finalized designs to the client as I have a background in front end development and computer science in general.

Accessibility/UX Guidance

As most projects overlapped greatly and had drastically varied timelines for final designs, I constantly acted as the go-to design resource as the lead UX/UI designer on CDPH. I would provide guidance not only on designs that I had created for various offices, but also for pre-existing systems that need UX guidance and previously designed systems that were looking for UX upkeep.

Within my specialization in accessibility, I also acted as a go-to accessibility resource, providing accessibility guidance, testing, evaluations, reports, and solutions on pre-existing and newly created products and features.

My guidance in both of these areas stretched beyond CDPH to numerous other state projects that came up in the timeline where time permitted me to help out and provide guidance.
