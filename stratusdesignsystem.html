Stratus Design System - Empyrean

Overview

As UX is a newly introduced discipline at Empyrean, many areas for improvement are still being uncovered. One of the most consistent pain points flagged by both users and clients has been the lack of consistency across Empyrean’s suite of products. To address this, I was brought on to lead the creation of a cross-platform design system, one that would not only drive visual and interaction consistency, but also improve accessibility, design efficiency, and scalability.

In addition to owning the UX design system strategy and component design, I also served as a product manager across product and engineering, helping to guide implementation, prioritization, and collaboration across teams.

***Note: Project is currently in development.

Target Users

  • All Empyrean clients and their employees

Key Contributions

  • Led UX strategy and execution for the creation of Empyrean’s first cross-platform design system
  • Designed 100+ reusable components, patterns, and libraries in Axure, Adobe XD, and Figma, with interactive prototyping and developer handoff functionality
  • Developed scalable design system documentation for both technical and non-technical audiences
  • Created and maintained a design component backlog and JIRA board to support cross-functional collaboration and progress tracking
  • Authored the long-term strategy for how the design system is created, maintained, and scaled across future products
  • Collaborated with engineering to define and deliver design tokens and provided accessibility guidance throughout design and implementation phases

Main Challenges

Fragmented Ecosystem Across Multiple Products

Empyrean had recently acquired another company and its application, which needed to be integrated into the existing product ecosystem. However, even within Empyrean’s native platforms, there was already significant inconsistency in UX and UI standards, making unification across products even more complex.

Shifting Timelines and Prioritization

The design system was not originally part of the 2024 roadmap. However, a surge in client feedback highlighting UI/UX inconsistencies led to a reprioritization, fast-tracking this initiative mid-year. This required rapid alignment across teams and balancing against existing commitments.

Client-Level Customization & Accessibility Conflicts

Empyrean allows clients to customize their platform’s color schemes to match internal branding. While this feature offers flexibility, it also introduced challenges around color contrast, accessibility compliance, and design uniformity, necessitating a new set of guidelines and system constraints within the design system work.

Full Process

Requirements Gathering

To establish a scalable and effective design system across Empyrean’s expanding product ecosystem, I began by collecting foundational insights to guide our approach. This initial phase focused on identifying opportunities, defining responsibilities, and repurposing any existing assets that could accelerate our progress.

Usability Analysis Across Products

I conducted a comparative analysis across Empyrean’s internal applications and the newly acquired software to identify the most frequently used components. This helped us pinpoint overlap and prioritize which components to build first based on usage patterns and impact.

Stakeholder Alignment and Process Definition

I facilitated early conversations to clarify stakeholder roles, release expectations, ownership at each stage of the process, and ideal handoff points between design and development. This ensured everyone was aligned before creation began.

Audit of Legacy Design System Assets

I reviewed a bare-bones design system that had previously been started but sidelined due to shifting priorities. From that audit, I identified reusable elements and lessons learned that could be folded into our renewed system strategy, saving time while avoiding earlier pitfalls.

Scope Definition

Following the requirements gathering phase, I led a project kickoff with key stakeholders to align on scope, ownership, and execution strategy. This meeting laid the foundation for how we would structure, scale, and sustain the design system moving forward.

Initial Component Prioritization

We identified the core set of components to begin with, focusing on those with the highest cross-product impact and technical feasibility.

Role Definition Across Functions

Clear responsibilities were established for UX, engineering, product, and other stakeholder groups to ensure ownership and reduce bottlenecks during design and implementation.

Scalable Strategy Framework

I introduced a scalable design system strategy tailored to Empyrean’s current product maturity and future growth. This framework provided guidance on system evolution, governance, and adaptability.

Communication Cadence

We defined key communication channels and a cadence of meetings, including weekly touchpoints with product and engineering, to maintain visibility, address blockers, and encourage cross-functional collaboration.

With alignment achieved, we transitioned into active component creation and system setup, ensuring each piece was designed with both present needs and future scale in mind.

Component Creation and Documentation

To date, our team has completed and released the first 11 foundational components of the design system across Axure and Figma (with Adobe XD now sunset as our focus transitions fully to Figma). Each component was built with scalability, accessibility, and cross-platform use in mind, undergoing multiple iterative reviews with product and engineering teams before formal handoff to development.

As engineering progresses through implementation, I facilitate weekly syncs to surface and resolve roadblocks, provide UX guidance, and ensure consistency between design intent and development output. We also host biweekly stakeholder meetings to collaboratively address higher-level issues and maintain transparency across disciplines.

In parallel, I continue to refine and prioritize the component backlog while proactively designing the next wave of patterns and templates. This includes developing Figma-based onboarding materials and prototyping resources to accelerate designer adoption as we complete the tool migration and consolidate post-acquisition workflows.
