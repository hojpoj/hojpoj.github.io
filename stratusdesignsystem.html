Stratus Design System - Empyrean

Overview

With UX being a very new addition to Empyrean, many improvements and opportunity areas are still being uncovered. One of the main areas consistently pointed out by users and clients is the lack of consistency across Empyrean products. As a way to standardize UX and UI across platforms, I was brought on to help stand up a design system to not only help with consistency, but also with accessibility and efficiency. I have been responsible for standing up, creating, and maintaining our design system from a UX perspective as well as acting as a product manager on the product and engineering sides of the projects as well.

***Note: Project is currently in development.

Target Users

  • All Empyrean clients and their employees

Deliverables

  • Design system components, patterns, and libraries designed in Axure, Adobe XD, and Figma with interactive prototyping capabilities included for our designers as well as created in a way that developers can use for reference during their creation processes
  • Design system documentation from both technical and non-technical perspectives
  • Design component/pattern backlog creation and maintenance as well as JIRA board creation and maintenance for tracking purposes
  • Design system strategy for how we work to create an initial design system, how we maintain in, and how we plan to keep it scalable as our products grow and expand
  • Design token creation for development
  • Accessibility recommendations/considerations

Main Challenges

Multiple Products

Empyrean had recently acquired another company/application that has to be accounted for in addition to Empyrean’s own products, which are also not consistent with each other currently

Rapidly Changing Timelines & Requirements

Creation of the design system was not originally slated for the 2024 roadmap, but as client feedback has come it, it has become reprioritized as a main priority for the rest of the year

Client Customization

Currently, clients are allowed to set their own colors to stay in alignment with their company colors which as led to some complications around usability and accessibility that are being addressed with the design system work

Full Process

Requirements Gathering

To understand how we could slowly start to implement a design system across all products, it was important to gather key information upfront. This consisted of:

  • Conducting a usability analysis across applications to understand what the most used components would be and matching them up with key components from the software recently acquired for another company.
  • Sitting down to define stakeholders as well as timelines expectations of how we would release components, where components should be designed, who would be in charge of each step of the process, etc.
  • Reviewing a small bare bones design system that had been worked on briefly, but set to the side due to priority shifts so that I could identify any work that could be utilized as we moved forward with standing up a full design system.

Scope Definition

After gathering requirements, I met with stakeholders for an initial kick-off meeting and we discussed the following:

  • The beginning components to be worked on
  • Responsibilities of UX, engineering, product, and stakeholders specifically
  • Solidified a design system strategy process I had created that could be scalable and adapted as needed
  • Channels of communication and meetings needed

With the details solidified, we moved onto the creation process of actual design system components as well as setting up kickoffs and weekly touchpoints with engineering and product.

Component Creation and Documentation

Currently, we are at the point where components have been created and released for the first 11 components of the design system from a UX perspective across Axure and Figma (we sunset our Adobe XD version as we mainly work out of Figma now). The component designs have gone through multiple reviews with product and engineering and have officially been handed off to engineering for development. As engineering works on development, we maintain weekly syncs to go over any roadblocks they encounter and need guidance on as well as maintaining a biweekly stakeholder meeting where we can bring bigger problems for discussion

While the main components are in the hands of engineering, I am proactively working on the next components being prioritized in the backlog as well as creating templates and prototypes in Figma to help easily transition our designers into the new design tool as we complete acquisition.
